Growth Guide

Grubhub, 2019

As our design team grew, the leadership team received feedback that designers didn’t know what to work on to develop their careers.

We needed more consistency in formal reviews, with clear guidance throughout the year. We also needed leveling guidelines to maintain objectivity, to be used as the source of truth in manager evaluations.

Outcomes:

  • Shipped a new Growth Guide, used by the entire Design org in performance reviews. Framework was applauded by HR, and adopted by the Product org for their own guidelines.
  • Introduced a “steps” framework to guide development between levels. Team members at each level would be evaluated into Learning, Proficient, or Excelling steps.
  • Clearly spelled out development into IC vs management career tracks, which was previously unclear.
  • Developed a worksheet for quarterly performance reviews. Designers would self-evaluate all performance criteria, a manager assessment would follow, and the two would review together in a 1:1. Over time, a designer would be able to see their growth through their worksheet history.

Design Critique Improvements

Grubhub, 2020

Our design team had a very loose critique structure that wasn’t serving us well. It was too unfocused, as we’d also use this time for announcements or random group activities.

We had a studio of 20–30 people cram into one room for one hour a week, with 2–3 people signed up to present work. This resulted in way too many people without context into the work. Presenters weren’t receiving enough quality feedback, and would often run out of time.

Outcomes:

  • Split into two, focused meetings.
  • Design Boost: A Monday morning “boost” to start the week, with focus on culture and announcements.
  • Critique Clusters: Split the studio into three critique groups with a mix of product designers, UX researchers, and content strategists, with group members rotating each quarter.
  • Post-release survey results demonstrated higher team satisfaction from Boost, and better feedback from Critique.
